# Sepsis — Hour-1 Bundle

Source: Surviving Sepsis Campaign 2026, CMS SEP-1

---

## SCREENING — bedside concern first

- Think sepsis when the patient looks sick: hypotension, altered mentation, tachypnea, or a clear infectious source.
- qSOFA is not the primary screening framework here.
- **SSC 2026 explicitly recommends NEWS2, MEWS, or SIRS over qSOFA** as the single screening tool for acutely ill hospital patients.
- Use NEWS2 (available in clinical-calculator skill) for structured screening: score ≥5 = urgent review, ≥7 = emergency response.

---

## HOUR-1 BUNDLE (clock starts at triage/recognition)

1. **LACTATE** — draw now. If > 2 mmol/L, clinical alert + remeasure in 2-4 hours.
   - Why we care: lactate = global tissue hypoperfusion. Watch urine output and ensure ordered BP parameters are maintained.
2. **BLOOD CULTURES** — 2 sets (aerobic + anaerobic) from 2 sites BEFORE antibiotics
3. **ANTIBIOTICS** — broad-spectrum IV within 1 hour of recognition. Every hour delay increases mortality ~7%.
   - **SSC 2026 update**: Prolonged beta-lactam infusion (extended or continuous) after loading dose is now a **strong recommendation** over intermittent bolus.
   - **SSC 2026 update**: De-escalate antibiotics when culture results and susceptibility data are available (**strong recommendation**, upgraded from 2021).
4. **FLUIDS** — anticipate 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension OR lactate >= 4 mmol/L.
   - **Fluid type**: Balanced crystalloids (e.g., Lactated Ringer's, Plasma-Lyte) preferred over 0.9% saline (SSC 2026, upgraded recommendation).
   - **Guidance method**: Use dynamic measures (passive leg raise, stroke volume variation, IVC collapsibility) over physical exam or static measures alone (SSC 2026, upgraded).
   - Assess volume status first — fluid-overloaded patient in respiratory failure is a different conversation. Per provider order.
5. **VASOPRESSORS** — if MAP < 65 after fluids: NOREPINEPHRINE first-line, target MAP ≥ 65 per provider order.
   - Note: SSC 2026 downgraded norepinephrine recommendation strength from strong to weak, but it remains first-line.

---

## REASSESSMENT TRIGGERS

- Lactate > 2 = clinical alert + remeasure
- Repeat lactate if initial > 2 (target: trending down)
- Reassess volume status if still hypotensive after 30 mL/kg
- Document reassessment: vital signs, physical exam, urine output
